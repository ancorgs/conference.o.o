openSUSE Conference přichází!

S radostí oznamujeme, že po prozkoumání mnoha úžasných míst, dlouhých diskuzích a změnách na poslední chvíli, konečně máme přesné místo a čas konání openSUSE Conference 2012 (oSC12) a naše CfP komise je připravena přijímat návrhy na přednášky!

location and co-location

This year oSC12 will take place at the Czech Technical University in Prague. The campus is located in the district Dejvice and is next to an underground station that gets you directly to the historic city center. The conference will start at October 20 and end on the 23th.

Like all openSUSE events, the Conference aims to bolster the 'open' in openSUSE, bringing together openSUSE contributors and members of numerous other Open Source projects. This years openSUSE Conference theme is "Bootstrapping awesome!". Why? Not just because of new new city and many new people on the organization part. But also because we are not alone! This year, we will co-locate with no less than three other events: the local LinuxDays, the largest Czech Free Software event; the first Gentoo Minisummit; and the 14th SUSE Labs conference. Having so many projects and people in one place will create a rich environment for fun and innovation, a place where ideas become reality and new friends are found!

Read more in our official announcement.

Call for Presentations

A Paper Committee has been assembled and is soliciting proposals for talks, workshops and presentations on a wide range of subjects. If you know about Packaging and the tools around it, are involved in core Linux infrastructure like kernel or userland tooling, hack on GNOME or KDE, are an experienced sysadmin or otherwise have something interesting to bring to the conference, go and read up on what they're looking for!

Sign up to be there!

If you want to join the openSUSE Conference, go and check out our awesome website. You can sign up there to receive regular information and hear when registration will open.

We expect to welcome about 500 Open Source developers, testers, usability experts, artists and professional attendees to the openSUSE conference. The entire combined conference is expected to attract well over 1500 people. Admission to the openSUSE conference as well as LinuxDays, the SUSE Labs conference and the Gentoo Summit is free. However for oSC a professional attendee ticket is available that offers some additional benefits.

About openSUSE

The openSUSE project is a worldwide effort promoting the use of Linux everywhere. openSUSE creates one of the world's best Linux distributions, working together in an open, transparent and friendly manner as part of the worldwide Free and Open Source Software community.

The project is controlled by its community and relies on the contributions of individuals, working as testers, writers, translators, usability experts, artists, ambassadors and developers. The project embraces a wide variety of technology, people with different levels of expertise, speaking different languages and having different cultural backgrounds.
