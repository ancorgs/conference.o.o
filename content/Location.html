Airport Information:

    Prague is served by a single international airport, the Prague Ruzyně International Airport, airport code PRG. The airport is served by many international carriers both inside and outside Europe. The Czech Republic is part of the Schengen zone, so travellers within the zone does not need immigration processing.

Alternate Transportation:

    The nearest train station is Hlavni nadrazi (Central station), which is the main train station. If you're going to use a train, this is the station where you want go. Then take the suwbay line "X" from plaform Y and go to the station Z (A stops, ~B minutes) from platform Y.

About Prague:

Everybody knows Prague is a beautiful city. It is the capital of the Czech Republic and a major European metropole. With about 2 million people in the area, it is the fourteenth largest city in the EU and is over a 1000 years old. In its history it has long been the political, cultural, and economic centre of central Europe and much of this is still visible in the beautiful buildings and the many museums.

Below is a list of some of the main attractions in Prague. Most of them are close by, all of them can be easily reached by the excellent public transport in Prague.

  • Prague Castle
  • Charles Bridge
  • Old Town Square
  • The Jewish Quarter
  • The Lennon Wall
  • Petřín hill
