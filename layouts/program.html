5 Days of Awesome Program
Speakers that blow your mind!

oSC13 has the most awesome program ever. You'll see rock-star speakers cover the topics of our community and technology. Strap in for a hefty ride through the oSC program roller coaster!

  • Georg Greve

    Georg Greve, founding president of the Free Software Foundation Europe and CEO at Kolab Systems AG, will keynote about Freedom in the world we live in and the value and importance of communities and Software Freedom. He'll show you Free in practice later during the event, giving a hands-on workshop around the Kolab Groupware Solution.

  • Henne Vogelsang

    Henne, founding member and developer of the openSUSE Project, will not only teach you in two hour workshops on how to utilize the most awesome Open Build Service to simplify your software packaging and maintenance needs. He'll also lead you down the rabbit hole into the wonderful world of Ruby on Rails web development.

  • YOU?

    Do you have something to say? About our community, our technology or Free and Open Source software in general? Our paper committee is ready to receive your proposals! For details on what we are looking for, see our Call for Papers. The submission deadline is June 17th. Share what you know and love, that's what it is all about.

  • Vincent Untz

    Vincent, involved in high-profile projects such as GNOME and OpenStack, and current board chair will lead the big openSUSE Town Hall Meeting and Team Reports sessions. If you want to discuss any project-wide topic, get to know what's happening, or need answers from the leaders of the openSUSE project -> This is your once-in-a-year opportunity, don't miss it!

  • Michal Hrušecký

    Michal, SUSEs SQL ninja and openSUSE Release Team member will present you an overview about the complex process of releasing the openSUSE Distributuon. What needs to be done to get a new version out and how you can pitch in to make it happen? Additionally he'll prepare you and your keys/certificates for the grand key signing bash and shares his experience on how to travel light.

  • Miguel Ángel Barajas

    Miguel, a senior cloud computing solution architect at HP, is most famous as the host of the spanish spoken podcast openenchilada. He's a Linux ninja with a black belt in openSUSE, virtualisation and cloud computing and his contribution to the program will be an introduction to the high availability (HA) technologies out there to create robust, mission critical services.

  • Alberto Planas

    Alberto, an openSUSE freshmen and new SUSE employee will explore the number of openSUSE downloads and installations and the evolution of these numbers. Does this data show a trend? Are we using this data to make important decisions about our future as a GNU/Linux distribution? Find out at oSC13!

  • Theo Chatzimichos

    Theo, a SUSE QA engineer, openSUSE & Gentoo Developer/Sysadmin will speak about using code for configuration management with Puppet. And if that wouldn't be enough, he also will hold your hand while you take your first steps with Puppet and learn it's declarative language, usage and advantages in a workshop.

  • Jos Poortvliet

    Community manager and long-term Free Software advocate, Jos has some thoughts he'd like to share in his keynote on collaboration in Free Software communities and why working together is better than going it all alone. He will also discuss release marketing, present the new merchandising and be part of the discussions around the changing openSUSE ambassador program.
